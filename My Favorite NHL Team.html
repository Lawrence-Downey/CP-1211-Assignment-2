    The (what is now) Toronto Maples Leafs, began their journey in the NHL back in 1918

         when they first joined the league as the Toronto Arenas. 
         (History of logos shown in the image above).



Every Maple Leaf fan has found themselves at the butt end of a joke at some point in their life. The reason being, that the Leafs (while icing extremely talented teams over the decades)
have not won a Stanley Cup since 1967. In fact, the last time a member of the organization hoisted the cup, televisions had no color.




    

    Toronto Maple Leafs win the Stanley Cup, 1967.



In the early years of the Leafs, owner Conn Smythe had a phrase that he believed in when building his team:
if you can't beat them in the alley, you can't beat them on the ice.
- Conn Smythe
